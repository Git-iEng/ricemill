Sequential stages for pre-cleaning and handling paddy before final milling.

Detailed Stages of the Pre-cleaner Rice Milling Process

Each stage below shows its role in the line and common machine types used in modern rice mills.

1

Pre-cleaning

Cleaner series is specially designed to separate oversized and undersized foreign bodies from food grains. Sealed housings minimise dust leakage and keep the milling floor clean.

2

De-stoning

De-stoners remove stones, glass and other heavy contaminants that are similar in size to paddy, protecting downstream machines and improving food safety.

3

Shelling

Rubber shellers de-husk paddy using two counter-rotating rolls. Correct pressure and speed control maximise brown rice yield and minimise broken grains.

4

Husking

Husk aspirators separate loose husk from the shelled mixture using controlled air flow. Clean separation improves downstream grading and reduces dust.

5

Separating

Paddy separators split the mixture of paddy and brown rice after husking, sending paddy back to the sheller and brown rice forward to whitening.

6

Polishing

USP series gently polishes the rice surface. A fine mist of air and water is introduced to give a smooth, glossy finish with minimal broken grains.

7

Grading

Graders separate rice into different fractions based on size, thickness or length, producing market-specific quality grades.

8

Storing

Intermediate bins and silos buffer material between sections, enabling continuous operation, stable flow and easier cleaning during shutdowns.
